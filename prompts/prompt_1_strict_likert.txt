Prompt 1: Strict Eligibility Screening — Likert Format

Purpose:
This prompt asks the model to evaluate how likely a study is to meet strict systematic review eligibility criteria.

Important note:
This prompt uses strict eligibility logic. A high score should be assigned only when the abstract and metadata strongly suggest that all eligibility criteria are met.

Study Details:
{study_details}

Screening Task:
You are an expert systematic review screener. Your task is to rate how likely the study is to be eligible for a systematic review of interventions for youth experiencing homelessness or housing instability.

Evaluate each criterion independently.

Eligibility Criteria:

1. Original or Empirical Research
The study should be based on original empirical research, primary data, intervention evaluation, implementation data, or direct observations.

2. Youth Population
The study should focus on youth, adolescents, young adults, or young people within or overlapping ages 13–25.

3. Homelessness or Housing Instability
The population should be homeless, unstably housed, runaway, street-involved, shelter-involved, couch surfing, doubled up, leaving foster care and at risk of homelessness, or otherwise clearly connected to homelessness or housing instability.

4. Intervention, Program, Service, or Policy Focus
The study should evaluate, describe, or examine an intervention, program, service model, policy, practice, or systems-level response.

5. Relevant Outcomes or Implementation Information
The study should report outcomes, effectiveness, implementation, service delivery, access, engagement, retention, participation, or other intervention-related evidence.

6. Relevant Country Context
The study should appear to take place in an OECD or comparable high-income country context.

7. Relevance to Youth Homelessness Intervention Evidence
The study should clearly contribute to evidence about interventions, services, systems, or programs for youth experiencing homelessness, housing instability, or clear risk of homelessness.

8. Sufficient Abstract Information
The title, abstract, and metadata should provide enough information to judge eligibility.

Scoring Instructions:
Rate the likelihood that this study meets the strict eligibility criteria:

1 = Very unlikely to meet eligibility criteria
2 = Unlikely to meet eligibility criteria
3 = Uncertain or partially meets criteria
4 = Likely to meet eligibility criteria
5 = Very likely to meet eligibility criteria

Use a strict interpretation.
Assign a high score only when most or all eligibility criteria are clearly met or strongly implied.
Assign a lower score when important criteria are missing, unclear, or not supported by the abstract.

Return only a JSON object in the following format:

{
  "Relevance_Score": 1,
  "Eligibility_Results": {
    "Original_Empirical": "Yes" or "No",
    "Youth_Age_13_25": "Yes" or "No",
    "Homelessness_Target": "Yes" or "No",
    "Program_Focus": "Yes" or "No",
    "Outcome_or_Implementation": "Yes" or "No",
    "Country_Context": "Yes" or "No",
    "Youth_Homelessness_Relevance": "Yes" or "No",
    "Sufficient_Information": "Yes" or "No"
  }
}

Return only the JSON object. No extra text.
