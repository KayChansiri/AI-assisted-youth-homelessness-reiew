Prompt 2: Screening Assistant / Triage — Likert Format

Purpose:
This prompt asks the model to rate how likely a study is to be relevant enough for human full-text review.

Important note:
This is not a final eligibility decision. Human reviewers will validate the final inclusion decision.

Study Details:
{study_details}

Screening Task:
You are assisting with title and abstract screening for a systematic review of youth homelessness interventions.

Your goal is to estimate how likely this study should move forward for human full-text review.

This is an initial triage stage. Do not require the abstract to prove final eligibility.

Pre-Screening Focus:

1. Original or Empirical Research
The study appears to use primary data, direct observations, evaluation data, implementation data, qualitative data, quantitative data, or mixed methods.

2. Youth or Young People
The study refers to youth, adolescents, young adults, young people, runaway youth, street youth, students, minors, transition-age youth, or mixed-age samples that may include youth ages 13–25.

3. Homelessness, Housing Instability, or Risk
The population is homeless, unstably housed, runaway, street-involved, shelter-involved, couch surfing, doubled up, leaving foster care, involved in homelessness services, or at risk of homelessness.
The study setting or system may also be connected to youth homelessness or housing instability.

4. Program, Service, Intervention, or Systems Focus
The study examines a program, service, intervention, policy, practice, outreach model, case management model, housing program, health service, school-based service, family intervention, behavioral health intervention, systems-level response, or other support relevant to homeless or at-risk youth.

Scoring Instructions:
Rate the likelihood that this study should be INCLUDED for human full-text review:

1 = Very unlikely to be relevant
2 = Unlikely to be relevant
3 = Possibly relevant
4 = Likely relevant
5 = Very likely relevant

Use the full range of the scale.
When abstracts are ambiguous or incomplete, assign a higher score if the study may plausibly be relevant.
Do not lower the score only because effectiveness results, implementation details, or full geographic confirmation are missing from the abstract.

Return only a JSON object in the following format:

{
  "Relevance_Score": 1,
  "Focus_Area_Results": {
    "Original_Empirical": "Yes" or "No",
    "Youth_Age_13_25": "Yes" or "No",
    "Homelessness_Target": "Yes" or "No",
    "Program_Focus": "Yes" or "No"
  }
}

Return only the JSON object. No extra text.
